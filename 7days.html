FROM THE DAY I SAW YOU

From the day I saw you, My heart began to race, My senses were awakened, And my soul began to chase. With just a single glance, You captured my attention, Your beauty, your grace, Left me in admiration.
From the day I saw you, My world began to spin, I couldn't believe my eyes, And didn't know where to begin. Your smile was like sunshine, Brightening up my day, Every step you took, Took my breath away.
From the day I saw you, I knew I was never the same, For your love had captivated me, And you were the only one to blame. Now, I sit here and marvel, At the journey we've been through, All thanks to that moment, From the day I saw you.

HER LAUNDRY

I fold her clothes with gentle care, Sorting them with loving stare. I want to make her life easier, My girlfriend whom I treasure. Her laundry basket seems so heavy, But I’ll carry it with ease gladly. I’ll make her favorite shirt clean, And her jeans as good as seen.
I’ll scrub her stains with patience, And rinse them with diligence. I’ll press them flat for neatness, All for her satisfaction and happiness. I’ll be her laundry helper, Her struggles, I will not defer. With me by her side always, Let her laundry worries be washed away. As clothes are hung and dried with care, Our teamwork proves that love is there. Through every task, big or small, Our bond grows stronger, climbing with each haul.
The scent of fresh laundry fills the air, And we bask in the comfort of knowing we're there. True love is shown in the smallest ways, And with this teamwork, our love forever stays.

I MISS HER

She had every intention to stay, I'm the one that pushed her away, Not heeding to a word she had to say, I should have listened, wasn't that hard anyway, I left her, now she's left me too, she's gone far away.

I thought i was strong, I didn't need her, i was better off on my own, The pain would ease away not after long, The wounds would heal and the feelings would wane before long, I had never been so wrong! I do miss her, though hard to admit, I miss the sound of her soft heartbeat, With her breasts pressed against my chest, I see her in my dreams when i go to sleep at night, Even when i open my eyes at first light.

I recall the memories we made, the dark secrets, Our wicked dance between the sheets, The wild orgasms and squirts, The soft moans that would then escape her lips, Trust me every shred of it hurts. So when you see her, tell her, i dont live life anymore, Tell her, my heart does not beat like before, Tell her, I'm not the same anymore, Tell her, i need her like never before, And the thought of losing her hurts me more.
